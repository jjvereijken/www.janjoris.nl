Operation Decibel

Introduction

On 10 October 1995, the dutch telephone network has undergone "Operation Decibel", an all-out "let's get it fixed once and for all" renumbering plan that affected the vast majority of dutch telephone numbers.

Since "Operation Decibel", all normal-rate telephone numbers are either in the format 0xx-xxxxxxx (for large cities) or the format 0xxx-xxxxxx (for smaller ones). In other words, all numbers now consist of 10 digits, hence the name "Decibel", and the effectuation date of 10-10-1995.

In the list given below, you can look up the "new" number for a certain subscriber, given the "old" number of that subscriber.

Click to jump to the areacode you're interested in:

  • Areacodes 010 to 01899
  • Areacodes 020 to 02999
  • Areacodes 030 to 038
  • Areacodes 040 to 04998
  • Areacodes 050 to 05999
  • Areacode 06
  • Areacodes 070 to 079
  • Areacodes 080 to 08897

Finally, note that telephones numbers starting in 010, 020, 0546, and 070 are the only ones that have not changed recently.

Areacode 06

"Areacode" 06 never was a proper areacode, but the prefix of a variety of non geographically-bound numbers. Amongst others, these included toll-free numbers, premium numbers, emergency numbers, operator services, directory inquiry, cellular phones, and pagers.

As of 1997 the 06 prefix has been reorganized, and split into 06 (mobile numbers only), 0800 (toll-free numbers), 0900 (premium numbers, informational), 0906 (premium numbers, sexually oriented entertainment, chatboxes etc.), 0909 (premium numbers, other entertainment), and some special numbers such as the supra-national European emergency number 112.
